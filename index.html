Systemic Findings
Leadership-level patterns across the full dataset
Critical — 100% Prevalence
Missing Action-Item Ownership Across All Evaluated Calls
Every single evaluated call — 65 of 65 — carries a flagged communication gap for missing explicit owners and/or deadlines on agreed action items. This is not an individual training gap. It is a structural process failure. AMs agree on next steps verbally but do not summarize them with names, dates, and accountability. "It'll go in Slack" is treated as a closure mechanism. Without a mandatory call-close SOP (owner · action · date), this remains the #1 systemic churn risk across the entire portfolio.
High Priority — Daniel Gonzalez
4 Alerts Including Two Ghost Calls — Pattern Requires Direct Intervention
Daniel has the highest absolute alert count (4 🔴) relative to his 14-call volume. Two alerts are ghost calls where no client showed up and no recovery action was taken — no agenda reset, no immediate client message, no reschedule confirmed. His Flow (3.5) and Expectations (3.1) scores are the weakest of active AMs. Strong relationship scores (4.6) mask execution gaps that are becoming a pattern. Leadership should review Daniel's call prep and ghost-call protocol directly.
High Priority — Expectations Dimension
"Expectations" Is the Weakest Dimension Across the Entire Team
Expectations management averages 3.6–3.7 across the team — the lowest of all 7 dimensions and where churn begins. Lumino complained about unresolved performance for months. Peppertree said projections were "planets apart." Flintts questioned ad spend ROI. AMs consistently score 4.5+ on Listening and 4.8+ on Relationship, but fail to set explicit, bounded, measurable expectations. Strong rapport is masking expectation gaps that clients eventually surface as dissatisfaction.
Risk — Compliance
Amazon TOS Violation Risk in Client-Facing Call
In the DNA Sellers call (Asif, Jan 5), multiple review-generation tactics — including friends/family reviews, insert-based incentives, and third-party review services — were discussed without a compliance disclaimer. These are flagged as high-risk or explicitly against Amazon TOS. If a client acts on this and the account gets flagged, AGL carries reputational and contractual exposure. A compliance review of how review strategy is framed in client calls is recommended.
Positive Signal — Protect This Baseline
Relationship & Listening Are Consistent Strengths
Despite structural gaps, AMs consistently score 4.5–5.0 on Relationship and 4.3–5.0 on Listening. Clients feel heard, they trust the team, and they regularly express genuine appreciation. "We are officially partners… we are teammates" (Snow), "That's better than we've ever done in January, by a wide margin" (Thrival), "You guys reply awesome… we appreciate you" (GoBrewing). This trust base is a real competitive asset — the QA system should elevate execution without eroding this warmth.
